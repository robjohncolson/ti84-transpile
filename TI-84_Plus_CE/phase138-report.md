## Findings

- The in-window cluster already contains real writes into the target neighborhood: `0x05690a` stores DE to `0xd0231a`, `0x05692a` stores HL to `0xd0231a`, and `0x056914` / `0x056925` store HL to `0xd0231d`.
- The adjacent helper at `0x056a02` is the tightest initializer-like block. It writes `0xd02317`, `0xd0231a`, and `0xd0231d` in one straight-line sequence, then rewrites `0xd0231a` after pointer arithmetic.
- The repeated `0xd0231a` stores in `0x059443-0x059cc4` are dominated by `ld (nn),bc` and appear behind token or state checks (`fe 10`, `fe 11`, `fe 2b`, `fe d0`). That looks more like runtime cursor/state maintenance than a one-shot boot initializer.
- The 7-byte lookup block at `0x0569a2` and the paired slots `0xd02317` / `0xd0231a` / `0xd0231d` point away from a flat callback array. This code behaves more like a small header for a moving window over records: base/start, current cursor, and end/limit.

## Hypothesis

- Most likely data structure: a compact pointer header over a packed stream or linked record area, not a pre-filled fixed jump table. `0xd02317`, `0xd0231a`, and `0xd0231d` behave like start/current/end slots.
- Most likely populator primitive: `0x056a02`. It performs the only straight-line multi-slot initialization in the immediate neighborhood.
- Best in-window entry point: `0x0569a9`, because it performs setup calls, invokes the thunk at `0x0569c8`, and that thunk tail-jumps directly into `0x056a02`.
